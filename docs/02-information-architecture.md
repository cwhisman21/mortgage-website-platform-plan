# Information Architecture

## Core Entities

The platform should be organized around entities and relationships, not hand-written isolated pages.

Primary entities:

- State.
- City.
- Branch or office.
- Loan officer.
- Loan product.
- Blog/news article.
- Calculator or tool.
- Market data source.
- CTA rule.
- Compliance disclosure.

## Public Site Areas

Recommended top-level navigation:

- Buy.
- Refinance.
- Home Equity.
- Rates.
- Calculators.
- Loan Options.
- Find a Loan Officer.
- Locations.
- Learning Center.
- Sign In.
- Apply or Get Prequalified.

## Page Relationships

Every major page should relate to other entities.

Example: Austin, TX city page.

- Parent state: Texas.
- Related branches: Austin branch, nearby branches.
- Related loan officers: Austin-based first, then Texas-licensed.
- Related products: FHA, VA, conventional, jumbo, refinance, home equity.
- Related articles: Austin market updates, Texas tax explainers, buyer guides.
- Related calculators: affordability, payment, refinance, rent-vs-buy.
- Related CTAs: get prequalified, find Austin loan officer, estimate payment.

## Content Graph Principle

Pages should cross-link based on structured relationships.

Examples:

- A Texas property tax article links to Texas, relevant cities, refinance pages, affordability tools, and Texas-licensed loan officers.
- A VA loan product page links to VA-heavy city guides, VA-specialist loan officers, and local VA buyer articles.
- A city page links to nearby cities, county/state guides, product pages, branches, and local news.

## Search Surfaces

Required search and discovery pages:

- Blog/resource search.
- Product search/product finder.
- Loan officer search.
- Branch/location search.
- City/state location directory.

Search filters should include:

- Keyword.
- State.
- City.
- Product.
- Borrower goal.
- Topic.
- Loan officer.
- Branch.
