# Vision And Positioning

## Product Thesis

The website should operate as a local mortgage intelligence platform, not a static mortgage brochure.

The core promise:

> Understand your local housing and mortgage market, compare your options, and connect with the right local loan officer.

## Audience

- First-time buyers.
- Move-up buyers.
- Refinancing homeowners.
- Home equity borrowers.
- Veterans and VA borrowers.
- Self-employed borrowers.
- High-balance and jumbo borrowers.
- Real estate referral partners.
- Loan officers and branch teams who need local placement and lead routing.

## Differentiation

Most mortgage websites lead with generic product claims, calculators, and application CTAs. This platform should lead with local intelligence:

- City and state market dashboards.
- Local news and editorial coverage.
- Data-backed buyer/refinance guidance.
- Location-aware loan officer and branch placement.
- Mortgage products connected to local facts and borrower intent.

## Strategic Outcome

The site should create qualified, educated borrower opportunities for loan officers and branches.

The user should leave each major page knowing:

- What should I know?
- What should I do next?
- Who can help me here?

## Non-Goals For Initial Planning

- Do not start with a generic marketing landing page.
- Do not make city/state pages thin SEO pages with swapped city names.
- Do not bury market data inside long-form copy only.
- Do not build a full mortgage application in this planning repo.
