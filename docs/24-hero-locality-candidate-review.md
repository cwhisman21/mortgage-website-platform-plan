# Hero Locality Candidate Review

Status: research candidate review, not asset approval  
Source access date: 2026-07-21  
Scope: four prototype state pages and twelve prototype city pages

## Decision boundary

This review records two rights-verifiable photographic candidates for every prototype locality. A **selected candidate** is only the preferred source for a future acquisition and rights-review workflow. It is not an acquired, approved, or publishable hero asset.

Every candidate remains:

- `acquisition_status: not_acquired`
- `rights_review_status: not_started`
- `publishing_status: prohibited`

The existing page-level hero records also remain `unselected`, `draft`, and prohibited from publishing. No image file was downloaded, copied into the repository, checksummed, or represented as reviewed. The links below go to the repository's individual asset record, not a search page or hotlinked derivative.

The research-selection bar is:

- city: exact geography, at least three independently visible locality-signal types, at least 2560×1440, and no unresolved release risk;
- state: exact or verified subregion, at least two visible locality-signal types, and enough breadth to avoid treating one region as the whole state;
- any identifiable person or distinctive private property without release evidence: blocked, regardless of visual quality;
- no generic fallback and no generated substitute when exact or verified-subregion photography exists.

## Set-level repetition and stereotype audit

This is a candidate pool, not a production-ready visual set.

### Repetition

- Nine of the ten selected city candidates contain a skyline. Seven also use a river, bay, or lake foreground. The result would feel formulaic if all ten shipped together.
- Austin is the only selection where housing and neighborhood fabric are primary. Denver includes a neighborhood-scale park, but its skyline remains prominent.
- No selected image carries a `lived_behavior` signal. The only strong lived-city candidate, Pearl Street in Boulder, is blocked by missing model/publicity-release evidence.
- Tony Webster would appear as the selected creator for both Dallas and Houston, with a third blocked Boulder candidate. A final set should avoid leaning on one photographer's visual grammar.

### Geographic and cultural stereotypes

- All four state pools remain unresolved because the available candidates collapse each state into scenery: Texas desert/hills, California coast/desert, Colorado mountains, and Florida wetland/barrier island.
- Florida's three city selections all use water as a defining foreground. That is locally defensible but overstates waterfront life relative to ordinary inland neighborhoods and housing.
- The Colorado state candidates reinforce a mountain-only identity; Garden of the Gods and Chautauqua create the same risk at city level. Their nonselection is intentional.
- Skyline imagery privileges downtown cores even though many mortgage decisions happen in lower-density neighborhoods. The pool needs commissioned or institutionally sourced neighborhood scenes with documented releases before a production set is approved.
- People are absent from every research selection. That reduces release uncertainty but produces an overly empty, observational view of place. Future sourcing should include release-cleared residents in ordinary local settings without using age, race, family type, home value, or visible disability as shorthand for a market.

### Production remediation

Before any set-level approval:

1. Commission or license at least one release-cleared neighborhood/lived-context option for every selected city now represented primarily by a skyline.
2. Build state-level multi-region composites only if every component has independent provenance and clearance; otherwise commission one broad editorial photograph or change the state hero treatment so it does not claim visual statewide representation.
3. Run a final side-by-side crop review at desktop and mobile ratios to remove water-plus-skyline repetition and test text-safe zones.
4. Have counsel verify creator identity, license version, required attribution, moral-rights/publicity/property issues, architecture/artwork treatment, and source-page stability before acquisition.
5. Preserve unresolved outcomes when the evidence remains weak. A blank asset slot is safer and more honest than generic stock or a visually plausible generated substitute.

## Exclusions

No candidates were sourced for `/`, `/locations`, `/calculators`, `/rates`, calculator pages, rates pages, or other protected page families. No generation was used because exact or verified-subregion photographs were found for every target geography; generation would not solve the unresolved breadth, specificity, or release problems.
