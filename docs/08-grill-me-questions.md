# Grill Me Questions

Use these to pressure-test the plan before implementation.

## Root Strategy

1. Is the public website primarily a Snap Mortgage acquisition surface, a Snap Homes consumer intelligence surface, or a hybrid?

Recommended answer: Snap Mortgage acquisition surface with Snap Homes intelligence hooks.

2. What is the first conversion object?

Recommended answer: an opportunity/lead routing event, not a full mortgage application.

3. What does success mean for v1?

Recommended answer: local pages and product pages educate users, capture intent, and route qualified opportunities to relevant loan officers/branches.

## Local Pages

4. What qualifies a city/state page for publication?

Recommended answer: enough unique market data, local facts, local LO/branch coverage, related products, and at least one useful local editorial angle.

5. Are city pages dashboard-first, guide-first, or news-first?

Recommended answer: dashboard-first with news and relevant editorial modules.

6. What data must be standardized across every city/state page?

Recommended answer: market snapshot, home price trend, payment scenario, tax/insurance table, related products, local experts, local news, FAQ.

## CMS/Admin

7. Who owns page content versus structured data?

Recommended answer: editors own copy and articles; admins/data managers own structured facts, charts, relationships, and CTA rules.

8. Can editors override automated relationships?

Recommended answer: yes, but overrides must be visible and reviewable.

9. What requires compliance approval before publishing?

Recommended answer: rates, payment examples, product eligibility, state-specific claims, AI-written local news, LO licensing claims, and any regulated advertising language.

## Loan Officer Placement

10. Why does a loan officer appear on a page?

Recommended answer: licensed state, nearby office/service area, product specialty, language fit, availability, and priority market assignment.

11. How should ranking work?

Recommended answer: city-based LOs first, then nearby branches, then licensed-state specialists, with admin override and audit trail.

12. Can a page show no loan officers?

Recommended answer: yes, but it should fall back to branch/company contact routing rather than fake local placement.

## Data And News

13. Which market metrics are automated versus manually edited?

Recommended answer: automate recurring metrics, allow manual overrides with source, date, and reviewer.

14. What is the minimum source standard for local news?

Recommended answer: every article must store source URLs, publication dates, location classification, and editorial/compliance review state.

15. How stale can market data be?

Recommended answer: each metric needs its own freshness rule; stale data should show last-updated context or be hidden.

## Technical Boundaries

16. Is this a separate repo from the existing central design prototype?

Recommended answer: yes for planning now; implementation should later align with the existing Snap ecosystem architecture and production backend decisions.

17. What should this repo produce next?

Recommended answer: PRD, wireframes, content model, admin model, and implementation issues before code.
