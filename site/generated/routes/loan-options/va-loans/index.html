VA Loans

Use the VA home-loan benefit with a complete eligibility and cost review

VA-backed loans can provide a distinct financing path for eligible Veterans, service members, and certain surviving spouses. A Certificate of Eligibility documents benefit information, but the lender still reviews the borrower and property. Entitlement, funding-fee treatment, value, closing costs, and occupancy belong in the comparison.

Use this VA Loans guide to organize the borrower, property, payment, cash, and timing questions that belong in a written comparison. Program availability and terms depend on the complete scenario and lender review.

Eligibility

Start with the Certificate of Eligibility and entitlement

The Certificate of Eligibility, often called a COE, records service-based eligibility and entitlement information used in the VA loan process. It is not a loan approval and does not establish a rate, payment, or affordable amount. The private lender separately reviews income, debts, credit, assets, intended occupancy, and the requested terms under VA requirements and its own applicable standards.

Prior use of the benefit can affect available entitlement. Ask the lender to read the current COE and explain whether entitlement is fully available, in use, or eligible for restoration. County conforming limits can matter in some remaining-entitlement calculations, but they are not a universal cap on every VA-backed loan. Use the actual purchase price, appraisal, entitlement, and lender analysis.

Costs

Review the funding fee, closing costs, and cash strategy

VA-backed purchase loans may permit no down payment when the borrower, entitlement, transaction, appraisal, and lender review support that structure. That does not mean no cash is needed. Closing charges, prepaid taxes and insurance, escrow funding, inspection costs, moving expenses, and the relationship between price and appraised value can affect funds required.

A VA funding fee may apply, and defined exemptions exist. Confirm exemption status through current records rather than assuming it. If the fee is financed, it increases the loan balance. Compare that treatment with cash payment and with other available financing. VA loans do not use monthly private mortgage insurance, but taxes, property insurance, association dues, and other ownership expenses remain part of the monthly budget.

Property

Plan for valuation, minimum property review, and occupancy

A VA-approved appraiser develops an opinion of value and reviews applicable minimum property requirements. The appraisal is not a home inspection. Buyers can separately consider an independent inspection and appropriate contract protections. If the opinion of value is below the purchase price or property conditions require attention, the parties may face additional decisions, documentation, repairs, renegotiation, or contract rights.

VA purchase financing generally includes occupancy requirements. Discuss timing and any unusual circumstances with the lender rather than assuming an exception applies. Condominiums, manufactured housing, multiple units, repairs, or other property features can involve additional program and lender review. Raise those facts early so the contract and closing schedule account for the actual property path.

Decision

Protect reserves while testing the full monthly obligation

The ability to use a smaller down payment can preserve liquidity, but borrowers should still decide deliberately how much cash to contribute. Compare the resulting balance, funding-fee treatment, rate, APR, payment, closing costs, and reserves. Include property taxes, insurance, association dues, maintenance, and location-specific ownership costs rather than evaluating the principal-and-interest amount alone.

Request written offers and compare them on consistent assumptions. Lenders set rates, points, and many charges, so the VA label does not create one universal price. Review the Loan Estimate and later the Closing Disclosure, ask about lock timing and conditions, and avoid relying on future refinancing. The benefit is most useful when its structure supports both the closing and a durable post-closing budget.

Questions to settle before you choose

Does a COE guarantee approval?

No. It documents VA benefit information. The lender still evaluates the borrower, requested loan, and property.

Does every VA purchase require no down payment?

No. Entitlement, appraisal, purchase price, lender review, and the chosen strategy can affect the down payment and cash needed.
