Champaign, IL housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Champaign, IL.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

Key takeaways

  • As of: 2024 ACS 5-year estimates. Source period: 2024.
  • total housing units: 41,299.
  • occupied housing units: 36,815.
  • vacant housing units: 4,484.

The dated finding: the size and use of the local housing stock

The published evidence provides the starting point: Champaign, IL's current ACS housing baseline includes 41,299 total housing units, 36,815 occupied units, and 4,484 vacant units. Those counts can help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 4,484 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. For borrower planning, reading 41,299 total units with 36,815 occupied and 4,484 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 36,815 occupied units in Champaign, IL, the ACS-derived owner share is 43.8% and the renter share is 56.2%, making renter-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. For borrower planning, the 43.8% and 56.2% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Champaign, IL. The 43.8% and 56.2% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon instead of used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Champaign, IL's ACS median gross rent is $1,123, while renter-occupied units account for 56.2% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 43.8%. In practical terms, the $1,123 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it cannot reveal a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,123 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. For the decision at hand, a more useful Champaign, IL comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

What the comparison adds: vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Champaign, IL decreased from 4,489 in the prior ACS period to 4,484 in the current release, a reported movement of 0.1%. In the current housing stock, 4,484 units equal 10.9% of the 41,299 total. ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 10.9% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. For the decision at hand, the movement from 4,489 to 4,484 can nevertheless prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Champaign, IL, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data instead of the survey count alone.

Evidence limits: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 10.9% vacant-unit share and 43.8% owner share cannot decide competition for a specific Champaign, IL listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply.

For the next comparison in Champaign, IL, likewise, 41,299 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 10.9% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. For borrower planning, borrowers should, as a result, keep 4,484 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,123 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Put the evidence to work: compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Champaign, IL comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 41,299 total units, a vacancy share of 10.9%, and an owner share of 43.8%. For renting, replace the $1,123 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes.

For borrowers reviewing evidence from Champaign, IL, on the ownership side, keep the 43.8% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. In practical terms, the resulting worksheet will not turn 56.2% or 43.8% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
