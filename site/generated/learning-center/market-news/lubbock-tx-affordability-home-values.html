Lubbock, TX home values, income, and owner costs

A borrower-focused reading of the latest ACS value, income, and owner-cost evidence for Lubbock, TX.

Evidence at a glance: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

Start with the dated local finding: In Lubbock, TX, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $212,400, median household income of $60,895, and median selected monthly owner costs of $1,794 for owners with a mortgage. Together, those three reference points may help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $1,794 measure combines recurring ownership expenses instead of real estate taxes alone. For borrower planning, a useful affordability review starts with $212,400, $60,895, and $1,794, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

How the city estimate sits within the state

Lubbock, TX's $212,400 median home-value estimate is below the statewide $283,800 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 25.2%.

That 25.2% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Lubbock, TX. It does not mean every local property is 25.2% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For borrower planning, a borrower can apply $212,400 and $283,800 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

Borrower view: read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $140,800 as Lubbock, TX's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $212,400. In practical terms, these dated nominal observations can still help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change.

Applied to a decision in Lubbock, TX, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $140,800 attached to 2019 and $212,400 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

Evidence limits: what the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $1,794 owner-cost estimate gives Lubbock, TX borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $60,895 income estimate supplies separate household context. In practical terms, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $60,895 is the owner household at $1,794. The $212,400 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. For the decision at hand, keeping $212,400, $60,895, and $1,794 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Keep the boundary clear: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $212,400 estimate nor the $1,794 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Lubbock, TX home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result. For borrower planning, the $60,895 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income.

In a borrower review centered on Lubbock, TX, the separate 2019 observation of $140,800 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. Those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Borrower action: build a property-specific comparison

A useful Lubbock, TX worksheet can begin with $212,400 as dated value context, $1,794 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $60,895 as a local economic reference. After noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves.

In a borrower review centered on Lubbock, TX, the 25.2% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $140,800 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. A licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
