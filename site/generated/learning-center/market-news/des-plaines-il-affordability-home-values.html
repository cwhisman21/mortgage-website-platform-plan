Evidence at a glance: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

Start with the dated local finding: In Des Plaines, IL, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $335,600, median household income of $97,875, and median selected monthly owner costs of $2,137 for owners with a mortgage. Together, those three reference points can still help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $2,137 measure combines recurring ownership expenses instead of real estate taxes alone. For borrower planning, a useful affordability review starts with $335,600, $97,875, and $2,137, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Evidence limits: what the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $2,137 owner-cost estimate gives Des Plaines, IL borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $97,875 income estimate supplies separate household context. In practical terms, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $97,875 is the owner household at $2,137.

Applied to a decision in Des Plaines, IL, the $335,600 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. For the decision at hand, keeping $335,600, $97,875, and $2,137 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $256,700 as Des Plaines, IL's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $335,600. In practical terms, these dated nominal observations can help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change.

Applied to a decision in Des Plaines, IL, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $256,700 attached to 2019 and $335,600 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

How the city estimate sits within the state

Des Plaines, IL's $335,600 median home-value estimate is above the statewide $263,300 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 27.5%. That 27.5% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Des Plaines, IL.

For the next comparison in Des Plaines, IL, it does not mean every local property is 27.5% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For borrower planning, a borrower may use $335,600 and $263,300 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

Keep the boundary clear: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $335,600 estimate nor the $2,137 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Des Plaines, IL home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result. For borrower planning, the $97,875 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income.

In a borrower review centered on Des Plaines, IL, the separate 2019 observation of $256,700 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. Those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Build a property-specific comparison

One practical Des Plaines, IL worksheet can begin with $335,600 as dated value context, $2,137 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $97,875 as a local economic reference. After noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves.

In a borrower review centered on Des Plaines, IL, the 27.5% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $256,700 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. A licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
