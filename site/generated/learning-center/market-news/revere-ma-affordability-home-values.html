Revere, MA home values, income, and owner costs

A borrower-focused reading of the latest ACS value, income, and owner-cost evidence for Revere, MA.

The dated finding: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

The published evidence provides the starting point: In Revere, MA, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $597,400, median household income of $86,969, and median selected monthly owner costs of $2,870 for owners with a mortgage. In practical terms, together, those three reference points may help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $2,870 measure combines recurring ownership expenses rather than real estate taxes alone. For the decision at hand, a useful affordability review starts with $597,400, $86,969, and $2,870, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Borrower view: read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $373,800 as Revere, MA's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $597,400.

For the next comparison in Revere, MA, these dated nominal observations can still help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change. For borrower planning, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $373,800 attached to 2019 and $597,400 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

Keep the boundary clear: what the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $2,870 owner-cost estimate gives Revere, MA borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $86,969 income estimate supplies separate household context. For borrower planning, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $86,969 is the owner household at $2,870.

In a borrower review centered on Revere, MA, the $597,400 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. Keeping $597,400, $86,969, and $2,870 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Read the comparison: how the city estimate sits within the state

Revere, MA's $597,400 median home-value estimate is above the statewide $562,100 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 6.3%. In practical terms, that 6.3% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Revere, MA. It does not mean every local property is 6.3% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For the decision at hand, a borrower may use $597,400 and $562,100 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

Evidence limits: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $597,400 estimate nor the $2,870 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Revere, MA home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result.

Applied to a decision in Revere, MA, the $86,969 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income. The separate 2019 observation of $373,800 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. For the decision at hand, those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Next step: build a property-specific comparison

A useful Revere, MA worksheet can begin with $597,400 as dated value context, $2,870 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $86,969 as a local economic reference. After noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves. The 6.3% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $373,800 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. For the decision at hand, a licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
