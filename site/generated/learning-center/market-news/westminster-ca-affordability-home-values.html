The dated finding: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

The published evidence provides the starting point: In Westminster, CA, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $858,300, median household income of $85,541, and median selected monthly owner costs of $2,869 for owners with a mortgage. In practical terms, together, those three reference points may help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $2,869 measure combines recurring ownership expenses in place of real estate taxes alone. For the decision at hand, a useful affordability review starts with $858,300, $85,541, and $2,869, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Borrower view: read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $603,700 as Westminster, CA's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $858,300.

For the next comparison in Westminster, CA, these dated nominal observations may help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change. For borrower planning, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $603,700 attached to 2019 and $858,300 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

Comparison view: how the city estimate sits within the state

Westminster, CA's $858,300 median home-value estimate is above the statewide $734,700 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 16.8%. In practical terms, that 16.8% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Westminster, CA. It does not mean every local property is 16.8% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For the decision at hand, borrowers can use $858,300 and $734,700 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

What the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $2,869 owner-cost estimate gives Westminster, CA borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $85,541 income estimate supplies separate household context.

In a borrower review centered on Westminster, CA, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $85,541 is the owner household at $2,869. The $858,300 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. Keeping $858,300, $85,541, and $2,869 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Where the evidence stops: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $858,300 estimate nor the $2,869 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Westminster, CA home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result. In practical terms, the $85,541 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income.

Applied to a decision in Westminster, CA, the separate 2019 observation of $603,700 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. For the decision at hand, those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Build a property-specific comparison

One practical Westminster, CA worksheet can begin with $858,300 as dated value context, $2,869 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $85,541 as a local economic reference. After noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves.

Applied to a decision in Westminster, CA, the 16.8% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $603,700 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. For the decision at hand, a licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
