Taylor, MI housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Taylor, MI.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

U.S. Census Bureau

Key takeaways

  • As of: 2024 ACS 5-year estimates. Source period: 2024.
  • total housing units: 26,537.
  • occupied housing units: 25,228.
  • vacant housing units: 1,309.

The dated finding: the size and use of the local housing stock

The published evidence provides the starting point: Taylor, MI's current ACS housing baseline includes 26,537 total housing units, 25,228 occupied units, and 1,309 vacant units. Those counts can nevertheless help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 1,309 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. For borrower planning, reading 26,537 total units with 25,228 occupied and 1,309 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Taylor, MI decreased from 1,970 in the prior ACS period to 1,309 in the current release, a reported movement of 33.6%. In the current housing stock, 1,309 units equal 4.9% of the 26,537 total.

ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 4.9% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. For the decision at hand, the movement from 1,970 to 1,309 can still prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Taylor, MI, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data instead of the survey count alone.

Evidence check: ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 25,228 occupied units in Taylor, MI, the ACS-derived owner share is 66.7% and the renter share is 33.3%, making owner-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. For borrower planning, the 66.7% and 33.3% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Taylor, MI. The 66.7% and 33.3% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon in place of used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Evidence check: use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Taylor, MI's ACS median gross rent is $1,101, while renter-occupied units account for 33.3% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 66.7%.

In practical terms, the $1,101 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it cannot identify a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,101 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. For the decision at hand, a more useful Taylor, MI comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

Evidence limits: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 4.9% vacant-unit share and 66.7% owner share cannot decide competition for a specific Taylor, MI listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. Likewise, 26,537 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 4.9% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. For borrower planning, a borrower should, as a result, keep 1,309 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,101 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Borrower action: compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Taylor, MI comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 26,537 total units, a vacancy share of 4.9%, and an owner share of 66.7%. On the rental side, replace the $1,101 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. For the ownership budget, keep the 66.7% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. In practical terms, the resulting worksheet will not turn 33.3% or 66.7% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
