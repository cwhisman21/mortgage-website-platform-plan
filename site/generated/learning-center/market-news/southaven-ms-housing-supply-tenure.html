Southaven, MS housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Southaven, MS.

Key takeaways

  • total housing units: 23,046.

  • vacant-unit share of housing stock: 7.8%.

The size and use of the local housing stock

Southaven, MS's current ACS housing baseline includes 23,046 total housing units, 21,251 occupied units, and 1,795 vacant units. Those counts describe the housing stock across the survey period; they do not say that 1,795 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. The difference between 23,046 total units and 21,251 occupied units is useful for checking the internal scale of the release, while 1,795 provides the numerator for a more careful vacancy reading. For a borrower researching Southaven, MS, the counts establish how large the measured housing base is before tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing questions are added.

Vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Southaven, MS increased from 1,386 in the prior ACS period to 1,795 in the current release, a reported change of 29.5%. In the current housing stock, 1,795 units equal 7.8% of the 23,046 total. ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 7.8% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. The movement from 1,386 to 1,795 can still prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Southaven, MS, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data rather than the survey count alone.

Ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 21,251 occupied units in Southaven, MS, the ACS-derived owner share is 72.5% and the renter share is 27.5%, making owner-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. The 72.5% and 27.5% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Southaven, MS. The 72.5% and 27.5% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon rather than used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Southaven, MS's ACS median gross rent is $1,445, while renter-occupied units account for 27.5% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 72.5%. The $1,445 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it does not identify a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,445 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. A more useful Southaven, MS comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

What the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 7.8% vacant-unit share and 72.5% owner share cannot determine competition for a specific Southaven, MS listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. Likewise, 23,046 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 7.8% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. Borrowers should therefore keep 1,795 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,445 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Southaven, MS comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 23,046 total units, a 7.8% vacancy share, and a 72.5% owner share. For renting, replace the $1,445 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. For ownership, keep the 72.5% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. The resulting worksheet will not turn 27.5% or 72.5% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
