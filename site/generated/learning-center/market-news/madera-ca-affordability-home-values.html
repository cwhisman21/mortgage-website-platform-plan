The local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

In Madera, CA, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $359,900 and median household income of $63,933; these are separate medians, not the finances of one representative household. The same release reports $1,851 in median selected monthly owner costs for owners with a mortgage, a measure that combines recurring ownership expenses rather than real estate taxes alone. For Madera, CA, reading $359,900, $63,933, and $1,851 together identifies the cost categories that deserve verification without converting public medians into a payment quote. An affordability review can start with the local $359,900, $63,933, and $1,851 reference points, then replace them with the borrower's income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

What changed between the ACS periods

Madera, CA's median home-value estimate increased from $224,100 in the prior ACS period to $359,900 in the current release, a reported change of 60.6%. Because each ACS 5-year estimate summarizes a multi-year survey window, the 60.6% comparison describes a change between published estimates rather than appreciation for a particular home. The move from $224,100 to $359,900 can help a borrower test whether an older search budget still reflects the broad local evidence, but it cannot identify a current asking price or future resale value in Madera, CA. Before the 60.6% change is treated as precise, the analysis still needs margins of error, property type, neighborhood, condition, and the timing of an actual transaction.

How the city estimate sits within the state

Madera, CA's $359,900 median home-value estimate is below the statewide $734,700 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 51.0%. That 51.0% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Madera, CA. It does not mean every local property is 51.0% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. A borrower can use $359,900 and $734,700 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

What the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $1,851 owner-cost estimate gives Madera, CA borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $63,933 income estimate supplies separate household context. The two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $63,933 is the owner household at $1,851. The $359,900 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. Keeping $359,900, $63,933, and $1,851 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

What these local medians cannot decide

Neither $359,900 nor $1,851 determines the value of a specific Madera, CA home, what a borrower will pay each month, or an underwriting result. The $63,933 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income. Even the observed 60.6% between ACS periods cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. Those boundaries keep the accurate $359,900, $1,851, and $63,933 statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Build a property-specific comparison

A practical Madera, CA worksheet can begin with $359,900 as broad value context, $1,851 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $63,933 as a local economic reference. After noting those $359,900, $1,851, and $63,933 medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves. The 51.0% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the 60.6% period change is a prompt to date every assumption. A licensed professional can use the 51.0% and 60.6% comparisons to explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices, and apparent changes may not be statistically meaningful without a margin-of-error test. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
