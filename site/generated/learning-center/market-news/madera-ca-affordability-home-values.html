Evidence at a glance: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

Start with the dated local finding: In Madera, CA, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $359,900, median household income of $63,933, and median selected monthly owner costs of $1,851 for owners with a mortgage. Together, those three reference points can help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $1,851 measure combines recurring ownership expenses in place of real estate taxes alone. For borrower planning, a useful affordability review starts with $359,900, $63,933, and $1,851, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Keep the boundary clear: what the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $1,851 owner-cost estimate gives Madera, CA borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $63,933 income estimate supplies separate household context. In practical terms, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $63,933 is the owner household at $1,851.

Applied to a decision in Madera, CA, the $359,900 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. For the decision at hand, keeping $359,900, $63,933, and $1,851 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Borrower view: read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $224,100 as Madera, CA's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $359,900. In practical terms, these dated nominal observations can help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change.

Applied to a decision in Madera, CA, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $224,100 attached to 2019 and $359,900 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

What the comparison adds: how the city estimate sits within the state

Madera, CA's $359,900 median home-value estimate is below the statewide $734,700 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 51.0%.

That 51.0% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Madera, CA. It does not mean every local property is 51.0% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For borrower planning, a borrower can use $359,900 and $734,700 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

Evidence limits: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $359,900 estimate nor the $1,851 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Madera, CA home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result.

In a borrower review centered on Madera, CA, the $63,933 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income. The separate 2019 observation of $224,100 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. Those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Borrower action: build a property-specific comparison

One practical Madera, CA worksheet can begin with $359,900 as dated value context, $1,851 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $63,933 as a local economic reference.

In a borrower review centered on Madera, CA, after noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves. The 51.0% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $224,100 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. A licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
