Garland, TX housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Garland, TX.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026

Key takeaways

  • total housing units: 86,216.
  • occupied housing units: 81,954.
  • vacant housing units: 4,262.
  • prior ACS vacant housing units: 4,034.
  • vacant-unit share of housing stock: 4.9%.

The size and use of the local housing stock

Garland, TX's current ACS housing baseline includes 86,216 total housing units, 81,954 occupied units, and 4,262 vacant units. Those counts describe the housing stock across the survey period; they do not say that 4,262 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. The difference between 86,216 total units and 81,954 occupied units is useful for checking the internal scale of the release, while 4,262 provides the numerator for a more careful vacancy reading. For a borrower researching Garland, TX, the counts establish how large the measured housing base is before tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing questions are added.

Vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Garland, TX increased from 4,034 in the prior ACS period to 4,262 in the current release, a reported change of 5.7%. In the current housing stock, 4,262 units equal 4.9% of the 86,216 total. ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 4.9% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. The movement from 4,034 to 4,262 can still prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Garland, TX, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data rather than the survey count alone.

Ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 81,954 occupied units in Garland, TX, the ACS-derived owner share is 61.6% and the renter share is 38.4%, making owner-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. The 61.6% and 38.4% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Garland, TX. The 61.6% and 38.4% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon rather than used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Garland, TX's ACS median gross rent is $1,641, while renter-occupied units account for 38.4% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 61.6%. The $1,641 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it does not identify a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,641 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. A more useful Garland, TX comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

What the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 4.9% vacant-unit share and 61.6% owner share cannot determine competition for a specific Garland, TX listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. Likewise, 86,216 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 4.9% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. Borrowers should therefore keep 4,262 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,641 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Garland, TX comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 86,216 total units, a 4.9% vacancy share, and a 61.6% owner share. For renting, replace the $1,641 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. For ownership, keep the 61.6% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. The resulting worksheet will not turn 38.4% or 61.6% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
