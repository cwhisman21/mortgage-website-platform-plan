Burnsville, MN housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Burnsville, MN.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

Key takeaways

  • As of: 2024 ACS 5-year estimates. Source period: 2024.
  • total housing units: 26,545.
  • occupied housing units: 25,891.
  • vacant housing units: 654.

The size and use of the local housing stock

Burnsville, MN's current ACS housing baseline includes 26,545 total housing units, 25,891 occupied units, and 654 vacant units. For borrower planning, those counts can help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 654 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. Reading 26,545 total units with 25,891 occupied and 654 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Planning context: ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 25,891 occupied units in Burnsville, MN, the ACS-derived owner share is 64.7% and the renter share is 35.3%, making owner-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. The 64.7% and 35.3% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Burnsville, MN. For borrower planning, the 64.7% and 35.3% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon instead of used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Burnsville, MN's ACS median gross rent is $1,513, while renter-occupied units account for 35.3% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 64.7%. For the decision at hand, the $1,513 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it cannot identify a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,513 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. In practical terms, a more useful Burnsville, MN comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

Comparison view: vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Burnsville, MN decreased from 1,054 in the prior ACS period to 654 in the current release, a reported movement of 38.0%. In the current housing stock, 654 units equal 2.5% of the 26,545 total. ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 2.5% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. In practical terms, the movement from 1,054 to 654 can still prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Burnsville, MN, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data instead of the survey count alone.

Evidence limits: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 2.5% vacant-unit share and 64.7% owner share does not determine competition for a specific Burnsville, MN listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. For borrower planning, likewise, 26,545 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 2.5% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. Borrowers should, as a result, keep 654 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,513 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Next step: compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Burnsville, MN comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 26,545 total units, a vacancy share of 2.5%, and an owner share of 64.7%.

Applied to a decision in Burnsville, MN, for the rental budget, replace the $1,513 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. On the ownership side, keep the 64.7% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. For the decision at hand, the resulting worksheet will not turn 35.3% or 64.7% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
