The dated finding: the local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

The published evidence provides the starting point: In Naperville, IL, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $540,200, median household income of $155,105, and median selected monthly owner costs of $3,154 for owners with a mortgage. In practical terms, together, those three reference points can help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $3,154 measure combines recurring ownership expenses instead of real estate taxes alone. For the decision at hand, a useful affordability review starts with $540,200, $155,105, and $3,154, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $416,700 as Naperville, IL's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $540,200.

For the next comparison in Naperville, IL, these dated nominal observations may help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence. They are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change. For borrower planning, each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $416,700 attached to 2019 and $540,200 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

What the comparison adds: how the city estimate sits within the state

Naperville, IL's $540,200 median home-value estimate is above the statewide $263,300 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 105.2%. In practical terms, that 105.2% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Naperville, IL. It does not mean every local property is 105.2% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. For the decision at hand, borrowers can apply $540,200 and $263,300 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

What the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $3,154 owner-cost estimate gives Naperville, IL borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $155,105 income estimate supplies separate household context. For borrower planning, the two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $155,105 is the owner household at $3,154.

In a borrower review centered on Naperville, IL, the $540,200 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. Keeping $540,200, $155,105, and $3,154 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Where the evidence stops: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $540,200 estimate nor the $3,154 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Naperville, IL home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result.

Applied to a decision in Naperville, IL, the $155,105 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income. The separate 2019 observation of $416,700 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. For the decision at hand, those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Next step: build a property-specific comparison

A practical Naperville, IL worksheet can begin with $540,200 as dated value context, $3,154 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $155,105 as a local economic reference.

Applied to a decision in Naperville, IL, after noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves. The 105.2% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $416,700 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. For the decision at hand, a licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
