San Marcos, CA housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in San Marcos, CA.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

  • vacant housing units: 1,207.

Evidence at a glance: the size and use of the local housing stock

Start with the dated local finding: San Marcos, CA's current ACS housing baseline includes 32,557 total housing units, 31,350 occupied units, and 1,207 vacant units. In practical terms, those counts can still help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 1,207 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. For the decision at hand, reading 32,557 total units with 31,350 occupied and 1,207 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Evidence check: use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

San Marcos, CA's ACS median gross rent is $2,348, while renter-occupied units account for 36.3% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 63.7%. The $2,348 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it does not reveal a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration.

Comparing $2,348 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. For borrower planning, a more useful San Marcos, CA comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

Comparison view: vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in San Marcos, CA decreased from 1,545 in the prior ACS period to 1,207 in the current release, a reported movement of 21.9%. In the current housing stock, 1,207 units equal 3.7% of the 32,557 total.

ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 3.7% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. For borrower planning, the movement from 1,545 to 1,207 can nevertheless prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in San Marcos, CA, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data instead of the survey count alone.

Evidence check: ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 31,350 occupied units in San Marcos, CA, the ACS-derived owner share is 63.7% and the renter share is 36.3%, making owner-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group.

For the decision at hand, the 63.7% and 36.3% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of San Marcos, CA. In practical terms, the 63.7% and 36.3% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon in place of used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Evidence limits: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 3.7% vacant-unit share and 63.7% owner share cannot decide competition for a specific San Marcos, CA listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. For the decision at hand, likewise, 32,557 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements.

For borrowers reviewing evidence from San Marcos, CA, the ACS period behind the 3.7% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. In practical terms, the borrower therefore should keep 1,207 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $2,348 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Put the evidence to work: compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded San Marcos, CA comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 32,557 total units, a vacancy share of 3.7%, and an owner share of 63.7%.

For the next comparison in San Marcos, CA, for the rental budget, replace the $2,348 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. For the ownership budget, keep the 63.7% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. For borrower planning, the resulting worksheet will not turn 36.3% or 63.7% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
