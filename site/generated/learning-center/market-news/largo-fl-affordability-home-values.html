The local value, income, and owner-cost baseline

In Largo, FL, the 2024 ACS reports a median owner-occupied home value of $229,100, median household income of $62,096, and median selected monthly owner costs of $1,655 for owners with a mortgage. For the decision at hand, together, those three reference points can help a borrower identify which search-price, income, and recurring-cost assumptions need current verification first. They remain separate medians, not the finances of one representative household, and the $1,655 measure combines recurring ownership expenses instead of real estate taxes alone. In practical terms, a useful affordability review starts with $229,100, $62,096, and $1,655, then replaces them with the borrower's documented income, cash, debts, property taxes, insurance, association charges, loan terms, and intended occupancy.

Planning context: read the ACS vintages as separate nominal observations

The 2019 ACS published $124,700 as Largo, FL's median owner-occupied home-value estimate, while the 2024 ACS published $229,100. For borrower planning, these dated nominal observations can help a borrower notice that a search budget built around the older release needs current property evidence.

In a borrower review centered on Largo, FL, they are not inflation-adjusted, and the ACS home-value methodology changed between these vintages, so the two dollar figures are not a like-for-like appreciation series and should not be converted into a precise percentage change. Each estimate also summarizes a multi-year survey window and carries sampling error. Keep $124,700 attached to 2019 and $229,100 attached to 2024, then use current listings, comparable properties, and financing disclosures for the decision at hand.

Keep the boundary clear: what the owner-cost estimate includes and excludes

The $1,655 owner-cost estimate gives Largo, FL borrowers a broader monthly reference than principal and interest alone, while the $62,096 income estimate supplies separate household context. The two medians should not be divided to claim that a typical household spends a particular share of income, because ACS does not say the household at $62,096 is the owner household at $1,655.

For the next comparison in Largo, FL, the $229,100 local home-value estimate adds market context, but it also cannot supply a down payment, interest rate, mortgage insurance amount, tax bill, or insurance quote. For borrower planning, keeping $229,100, $62,096, and $1,655 in their published roles prevents a local affordability worksheet from acquiring false precision before a property and borrower are known.

Read the comparison: how the city estimate sits within the state

Largo, FL's $229,100 median home-value estimate is below the statewide $359,000 estimate, with a reported city-to-state gap of 36.2%. For the decision at hand, that 36.2% gap is useful for showing that a statewide midpoint may be a poor substitute for a local search range in Largo, FL.

For borrowers reviewing evidence from Largo, FL, it does not mean every local property is 36.2% away from a comparable property elsewhere in the state, because the medians reflect different housing mixes and geographies. In practical terms, borrowers may use $229,100 and $359,000 to decide which assumptions need local replacement first, then compare actual properties and current financing disclosures on the same basis.

Evidence limits: what these local medians cannot decide

Neither the 2024 $229,100 estimate nor the $1,655 owner-cost median determines the value of a specific Largo, FL home, a borrower's payment, or an underwriting result. For the decision at hand, the $62,096 median describes the local household distribution, not the stability, continuity, documentation, or eligible amount of an applicant's income. The separate 2019 observation of $124,700 also cannot forecast the next market move or establish a safe purchase horizon. In practical terms, those boundaries keep the published value, owner-cost, and income statistics from being misused as an appraisal, rate quote, underwriting result, or recommendation about how much debt one household should carry.

Put the evidence to work: build a property-specific comparison

One practical Largo, FL worksheet can begin with $229,100 as dated value context, $1,655 as a reminder to include recurring ownership expenses, and $62,096 as a local economic reference. After noting those published medians, substitute a real property price, down-payment plan, current loan disclosures, taxes, insurance, association obligations, maintenance allowance, and cash reserves. The 36.2% city-to-state difference is a prompt to keep the analysis local, while the separate $124,700 2019 observation is a prompt to date every assumption without calculating a cross-vintage growth rate. In practical terms, a licensed professional can explain which inputs are verified, which may change, and which loan options fit the documented borrower and property without presenting the ACS figures as an outcome.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 and 2019 ACS 5-year estimates for the Census place and a 2024 parent-state comparison, including published margins of error. The two home-value vintages are retained as separate nominal observations rather than converted into a percentage change. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS estimates represent survey periods rather than point-in-time prices. The 2019 and 2024 dollar values are not inflation-adjusted, and the home-value methodology changed between vintages, so they are not a like-for-like appreciation series. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
