What changed in the latest month

As of May 2026, Yuma, AZ's unemployment rate rose from 9.8% in April 2026 to 11.1% in May 2026, a change of 1.3 percentage points. For the decision at hand, that movement may matter when a borrower is pressure-testing timing, reserves, or the documentation needed after a job or income change. Over the year ending May 2026, Yuma, AZ's unemployment rate rose from 10.0% in May 2025 to 11.1%, a change of 1.1 percentage points. In practical terms, that wider window helps show whether Yuma, AZ's latest monthly movement sits inside a longer pattern, while the one-month movement of 1.3 percentage points answers a shorter timing question. Neither comparison predicts the next labor-market release for Yuma, AZ or describes the stability of one household's earnings. For mortgage planning in Yuma, AZ, the practical consequence is to date broad economic assumptions and then return to current pay history, variable compensation, self-employment records, contracts, leave, job changes, assets, and debts. Those borrower records carry more weight in a loan review than movement in Yuma, AZ's broad labor-market estimates.

Borrower view: why the rate needs its underlying counts

At 11.1%, Yuma, AZ's unemployment rate summarizes 5,019 unemployed people within a labor force of 45,035; the rate should not be discussed without those counts.

For borrowers reviewing evidence from Yuma, AZ, the 40,016 employment estimate is an economic measure and does not distinguish borrowers by hours, tenure, pay structure, industry, or the documentation available for a loan file. That makes 40,016 useful for local context but unsuitable as evidence that one household's income is stable or sufficient. In practical terms, keeping the 11.1% rate attached to 45,035, 40,016, and 5,019 prevents a small percentage movement from carrying more borrower-level meaning than the LAUS release supports.

The dated finding: the counts behind the published rate

For the May 2026 source period, BLS estimated 45,035 people in the Yuma, AZ labor force, including 40,016 employed and 5,019 unemployed under LAUS definitions.

In a borrower review centered on Yuma, AZ, the employment and unemployment counts belong together because they establish the scale behind the published 11.1% rate. In mortgage planning, that city-level labor force describes the surrounding economy rather than the income or employment record of an applicant. The counts should, as a result, follow the change summary instead of replacing the borrower answer.

Evidence limits: labor statistics are not an income review

Neither 40,016 employed people nor an unemployment rate of 11.1% tells a lender what an applicant in Yuma, AZ earns, whether that income is eligible, or whether it is likely to continue.

Applied to a decision in Yuma, AZ, the monthly movement of 1.3 percentage points also cannot substitute for pay stubs, tax returns, business records, contracts, benefit documentation, or an explanation of a recent job transition. The 11.1% rate can be relevant to timing and local economic questions while remaining outside the underwriting decision. For the decision at hand, that boundary protects borrowers from treating the year-over-year movement of 1.1 percentage points in either direction as an approval signal or as a conclusion about their own documented capacity.

Methodology

This article uses BLS LAUS unadjusted city monthly series for labor force, employment, unemployment, and unemployment rate, with prior-month and year-ago comparisons. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

LAUS estimates can be revised, city series are unadjusted, and local labor statistics describe a population rather than an individual applicant's income or employment. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
