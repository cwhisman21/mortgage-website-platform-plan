Trenton, NJ housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Trenton, NJ.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

U.S. Census Bureau

Key takeaways

  • As of: 2024 ACS 5-year estimates. Source period: 2024.
  • total housing units: 34,513.
  • occupied housing units: 32,052.
  • vacant housing units: 2,461.

Start with the evidence: the size and use of the local housing stock

The local figures answer the first question: Trenton, NJ's current ACS housing baseline includes 34,513 total housing units, 32,052 occupied units, and 2,461 vacant units. For the decision at hand, those counts may still help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 2,461 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. In practical terms, reading 34,513 total units with 32,052 occupied and 2,461 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Trenton, NJ decreased from 7,076 in the prior ACS period to 2,461 in the current release, a reported movement of 65.2%. In the current housing stock, 2,461 units equal 7.1% of the 34,513 total. ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 7.1% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. The movement from 7,076 to 2,461 can nevertheless prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Trenton, NJ, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data in place of the survey count alone.

Borrower view: ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 32,052 occupied units in Trenton, NJ, the ACS-derived owner share is 37.9% and the renter share is 62.1%, making renter-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group. In practical terms, the 37.9% and 62.1% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage.

This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Trenton, NJ. For the decision at hand, the 37.9% and 62.1% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon rather than used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Evidence limits: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 7.1% vacant-unit share and 37.9% owner share cannot determine competition for a specific Trenton, NJ listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply.

Applied to a decision in Trenton, NJ, likewise, 34,513 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements. The ACS period behind the 7.1% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. For the decision at hand, borrowers therefore should keep 2,461 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,294 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Evidence check: use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Trenton, NJ's ACS median gross rent is $1,294, while renter-occupied units account for 62.1% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 37.9%. For borrower planning, the $1,294 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it does not identify a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration. Comparing $1,294 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. A more useful Trenton, NJ comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

Put the evidence to work: compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Trenton, NJ comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 34,513 total units, a vacancy share of 7.1%, and an owner share of 37.9%. For the rental budget, replace the $1,294 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes.

In a borrower review centered on Trenton, NJ, for ownership, keep the 37.9% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. The resulting worksheet will not turn 62.1% or 37.9% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
