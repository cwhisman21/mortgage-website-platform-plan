Irving, TX housing supply, ownership, and rent

What the latest ACS housing mix can and cannot tell buyers and renters comparing options in Irving, TX.

Maya Brooks Snap Mortgage Editorial Contributor Last updated July 10, 2026 Snap editorial voice, not a loan officer or licensed mortgage professional.

  • vacant housing units: 7,667.

The dated finding: the size and use of the local housing stock

The published evidence provides the starting point: Irving, TX's current ACS housing baseline includes 102,512 total housing units, 94,845 occupied units, and 7,667 vacant units. Those counts can help a borrower establish the scale of the measured housing base before comparing tenure, rent, current listings, property condition, and financing. The figures describe stock across the survey period; they do not say that 7,667 homes were listed for sale or available to rent at one moment. For borrower planning, reading 102,512 total units with 94,845 occupied and 7,667 vacant units keeps the release internally grounded while leaving live availability to current listing and property evidence.

Planning context: ownership and renting within occupied housing

Among 94,845 occupied units in Irving, TX, the ACS-derived owner share is 38.1% and the renter share is 61.9%, making renter-occupied housing the larger measured tenure group.

For borrower planning, the 38.1% and 61.9% figures describe how occupied units were distributed; they do not measure how many households are ready to buy, how many owners plan to sell, or which properties can qualify for a mortgage. This local tenure mix helps a borrower avoid assuming that one housing path dominates every segment of Irving, TX. The 38.1% and 61.9% mix is most useful when paired with actual choices at a similar property type, location, condition, and time horizon in place of used as a signal to buy or rent by itself.

Borrower view: use rent as a matched comparison, not a shortcut

Irving, TX's ACS median gross rent is $1,619, while renter-occupied units account for 61.9% of occupied housing and owner-occupied units account for 38.1%. In practical terms, the $1,619 median includes rent and certain utilities for the measured rental stock; it cannot reveal a lease offer comparable to a home under consideration.

Comparing $1,619 with only a mortgage's principal and interest would omit taxes, insurance, maintenance, association charges, transaction costs, and differences between the properties. For the decision at hand, a more useful Irving, TX comparison matches real lease and ownership choices over the same expected horizon, then treats the tenure shares as market context instead of a break-even calculation.

Read the comparison: vacancy changed, but it is not listing inventory

Vacant units in Irving, TX increased from 6,098 in the prior ACS period to 7,667 in the current release, a reported movement of 25.7%. In the current housing stock, 7,667 units equal 7.5% of the 102,512 total.

ACS vacancy covers several statuses, so the 7.5% share cannot be read as the share of homes a buyer can purchase today or the share of rentals immediately available. For the decision at hand, the movement from 6,098 to 7,667 can still prompt better questions about current for-sale and rental inventory in Irving, TX, provided those questions are answered with timely listing and property data instead of the survey count alone.

Keep the boundary clear: what the housing-stock evidence cannot show

The 7.5% vacant-unit share and 38.1% owner share cannot decide competition for a specific Irving, TX listing, seller flexibility, inspection risk, appraisal results, insurance availability, or future supply. Likewise, 102,512 total units say nothing about how many properties fit a borrower's price, location, accessibility, unit-count, or condition requirements.

For the next comparison in Irving, TX, the ACS period behind the 7.5% vacancy share can accurately describe the broad stock while lagging a fast-moving listing market. For borrower planning, a borrower should, as a result, keep 7,667 vacant units separate from active inventory and keep the $1,619 median separate from a current lease quote, using each figure only for the question its definition can answer.

Compare current local choices on the same terms

A grounded Irving, TX comparison starts with current listings and lease offers, then uses the ACS evidence to check whether the sample is being interpreted in the context of 102,512 total units, a vacancy share of 7.5%, and an owner share of 38.1%. On the rental side, replace the $1,619 median with the actual lease, utilities, deposits, and expected changes. On the ownership side, keep the 38.1% share as market context while adding the selected property's price, taxes, insurance, maintenance, association obligations, loan disclosures, and cash-to-close. In practical terms, the resulting worksheet will not turn 61.9% or 38.1% into a recommendation, but it will keep the two paths comparable and make the remaining assumptions visible before a decision.

Methodology

This article uses 2024 ACS 5-year place estimates for units, occupancy, vacancy, owner and renter tenure, and gross rent, with 2019 evidence retained for comparison review. Figures are reported as published for each cited geography and period. Derived percentages and differences use only the displayed source values; unavailable figures are omitted rather than estimated.

What this data cannot determine

ACS vacancy includes categories beyond for-sale availability, and tenure and rent estimates carry sampling error and do not measure current listing inventory. The evidence is broad market context, not a property valuation, rate or payment quote, eligibility finding, underwriting decision, product recommendation, or prediction.
